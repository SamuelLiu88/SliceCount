🍕 About This App
Why I Built This

When eating lunch at school, I noticed that there was either a lot of leftovers or not enough food to satisfy the students. I realized that having something to help predict the amount of food eaten per lunch would be efficient and environmentally friendly which led to my making of an app that uses an A.I. to predict food consumption. At the high school, the main manager of the Nutrition Service Team was eager to find a solution to further reducing the waste of food. He kept tabs of how many meals were sold at each station and how much money was earned. I approached him with my solution for meal prediction, and he agreed to supply the data for my app.

What you'll need to run a prediction
  • 📂
    Meal history CSV files (last school year) One or more CSV files exported from your nutrition service system. Each file covers one station and meal type. Grill Breakfast.csv NC Lunch.csv Totals Breakfast.csv Don't have your own data yet? Load Camas SD sample data →
  • 📋
    Required columns inside each CSV A Date column (M/D/YYYY format) and a meals count column (any name containing "Meal" or "Total"). Zero values on non-school days are fine — the app ignores them automatically.
  • 📅
    Last year's school calendar You'll enter the school year start & end date, all holidays and breaks, and early-release days. Camas SD 2025–2026 dates are pre-filled as a starting point.
  • 🗓️
    This year's school calendar Same information for the year you want predictions for. Camas SD 2026–2027 dates are pre-filled and editable.
  • ⬇️
    Output A predicted meal count for every school day this year, shown as a chart and a table you can download as a CSV file.
